# 2026-05-03

## Full-Dataset K-Method Benchmark

Created a new isolated benchmark folder:

```text
msd_deezer_workspace/full_k_method_benchmark/
```

Purpose: decide how to choose the number of KMeans clusters for the full
catalogue after the first 10k test showed that `pretrained_embeddings +
PCA -> UMAP` is the best representation.

## Why This Benchmark Exists

The first 10k K-selection run showed a consistent disagreement:

- gap statistic selected many fine-grained clusters
- prediction strength selected fewer broad stable clusters
- pretrained embeddings with PCA -> UMAP had the best overall quality

Therefore the full-dataset decision should not be based on one sample. The new
benchmark repeats the K-selection process across multiple copied random samples
and separately validates a short list of practical candidate K values.

## Isolation Rules

The user explicitly requested that the production dataset stay untouched. The
benchmark follows that rule:

- Production audio under `msd_deezer_workspace/audio/` is read-only.
- Sampled audio is hard-copied into:

```text
full_k_method_benchmark/samples/<sample_id>/audio/
```

- Each sample workspace receives copied versions of the needed scripts and
  packages.
- All preprocessing, extraction, clustering, logs, and reports stay inside
  `full_k_method_benchmark/`.
- Safety guards reject writes outside the benchmark/sample roots.

## Default Samples

The benchmark picks five independent 10k samples:

```text
sample_10k_seed101
sample_10k_seed202
sample_10k_seed303
sample_10k_seed404
sample_10k_seed505
```

This is deliberately larger than the single 10k test but still much cheaper
than running gap statistic on the entire audio catalogue.

## Per-Sample Pipeline

For each copied sample:

1. `preprocess_downloaded_audio.py`
2. `run_parallel_extraction.sh 16`
3. `run_kmeans_gap_stability.py`
   - feature source: `pretrained_embeddings`
   - reduction: `pca_then_umap`
   - max K: `100`
   - workers: `16`
4. `run_candidate_k_validation.py`
   - candidate K values: `5, 8, 10, 12, 15, 20, 30, 40, 50, 60, 80, 100`
   - workers: `16`

## What Gets Compared

The benchmark separates two questions:

1. Which K does each selection method suggest repeatedly?
   - gap statistic one-standard-error K
   - prediction-strength stable K
   - best silhouette K
   - best Calinski-Harabasz K
   - best Davies-Bouldin K

2. Which practical candidate K is stable enough to use on the full catalogue?
   - bootstrap ARI stability
   - silhouette
   - Davies-Bouldin
   - cluster-size distribution
   - tiny-cluster counts

## Intended Interpretation

The final result should not be phrased as "the dataset truly has exactly K
clusters." Music recommendation data is expected to be continuous and
hierarchical.

The benchmark is designed to produce:

- a broad stable K range from prediction strength
- a fine-grained K range from gap statistic
- a practical full-catalogue candidate K from repeated candidate validation

The recommended production K should be the largest candidate that remains
stable enough and does not create tiny, unusable clusters.
